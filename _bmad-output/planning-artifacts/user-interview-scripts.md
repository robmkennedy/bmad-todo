## Interview Guidelines

### Before the Interview

| Preparation | Details |
|-------------|---------|
| **Duration** | 30-45 minutes |
| **Recording** | Request permission to record audio |
| **Environment** | Quiet, comfortable, no interruptions |
| **Materials** | This script, notepad, recording device |
| **Prototype** | Have BMad Todo open (or mockup ready) |

### Interview Best Practices

| Do | Don't |
|----|-------|
| ✅ Ask open-ended questions | ❌ Lead the witness |
| ✅ Follow up with "Tell me more" | ❌ Suggest answers |
| ✅ Embrace awkward silences | ❌ Fill pauses with your thoughts |
| ✅ Ask for specific examples | ❌ Accept vague generalities |
| ✅ Note body language/emotion | ❌ Only capture words |
| ✅ Stay curious and neutral | ❌ Defend or explain the product |

### Key Phrases

- "Can you tell me more about that?"
- "What do you mean by [term they used]?"
- "Can you walk me through the last time that happened?"
- "How did that make you feel?"
- "What would have made that better?"

## Script A: Busy Professional (Maya Archetype)

### Participant Profile
- Fast-paced work environment
- Multiple responsibilities
- Tech-comfortable but time-poor
- Uses mobile and desktop

### Duration: 35 minutes

---

### Section 1: Warm-Up (5 minutes)

*Goal: Build rapport, understand context*

> "Thank you for taking the time to speak with me today. I'm researching how busy professionals manage their tasks. There are no right or wrong answers — I'm just interested in learning about your experience."

**Questions:**

1. **"Tell me a little about your role and what a typical day looks like for you."**
   - Listen for: Context switching, meeting load, pace
   - Note: Energy level, stress indicators

2. **"How would you describe your relationship with productivity and task management?"**
   - Listen for: Frustrations, aspirations, self-perception
   - Follow-up: "Has that always been the case?"

---

### Section 2: Current Behavior (10 minutes)

*Goal: Understand existing habits and pain points*

3. **"Walk me through how you currently keep track of things you need to do."**
   - Listen for: Tools, systems, workarounds
   - Follow-up: "Show me if you can" (if remote: describe the tool)

4. **"Think about the last time you needed to capture a task quickly. What happened?"**
   - Listen for: Speed, friction, context (where/when)
   - Follow-up: "How long did that take?"

5. **"What happens to tasks you capture? How do you get back to them?"**
   - Listen for: Review habits, completion patterns
   - Follow-up: "How often do tasks slip through the cracks?"

6. **"What's the most frustrating thing about how you manage tasks today?"**
   - Listen for: Specific pain points
   - Follow-up: "Tell me about a time when that really affected you."

**Hypothesis Validation:**

| Hypothesis | Question to Ask | Signal to Watch |
|------------|-----------------|-----------------|
| Needs < 10 second capture | Q4 | Mentions speed, interruptions |
| Uses multiple devices | Q3 | Switches between mobile/desktop |
| Current tools too complex | Q6 | Complains about features, overhead |
| Loses tasks in multiple places | Q5 | Mentions sticky notes, scattered notes |

---

### Section 3: Task Capture Deep Dive (8 minutes)

*Goal: Understand the capture moment in detail*

7. **"When during the day do you most often need to capture a task?"**
   - Listen for: Meeting context, walking, between activities
   - Follow-up: "What device do you typically have at that moment?"

8. **"Describe the ideal task capture experience. What would it look like?"**
   - Listen for: Speed, simplicity, accessibility
   - Note: Emotional language (relieved, calm, confident)

9. **"Have you ever NOT captured a task because it felt like too much effort?"**
   - Listen for: Friction points, lost tasks
   - Follow-up: "What happened as a result?"

10. **"How important is it for your tasks to sync across your devices?"**
    - Listen for: Mobile/desktop workflow
    - Follow-up: "Tell me about a time that was a problem."

---

### Section 4: Tool Evaluation (7 minutes)

*Goal: Understand tool preferences and deal-breakers*

11. **"What task management tools have you tried? What did you think of them?"**
    - Listen for: Specific tools, likes, dislikes
    - Create a quick "tried / liked / disliked" list

12. **"If I gave you a new task app right now, what would make you close it immediately?"**
    - Listen for: Deal-breakers, red flags
    - Follow-up: "What would make you keep using it?"

13. **"How do you feel about apps that require you to create an account before you can use them?"**
    - Listen for: Friction tolerance, privacy concerns
    - Follow-up: "What if you could just start using it immediately?"

---

### Section 5: Concept Reaction (Optional - 5 minutes)

*Goal: Test BMad Todo concept if prototype available*

> "I'd like to show you a simple tool we're working on. Don't worry about being polite — honest feedback helps us make it better."

*Show BMad Todo or mockup*

14. **"What's your first impression?"**
    - Note: Time to understand, emotional reaction
    - Follow-up: "What do you think this does?"

15. **"Go ahead and try adding a task."**
    - Note: Time to completion, confusion points
    - Follow-up: "How did that feel?"

16. **"Would you use this? Why or why not?"**
    - Listen for: Honest assessment
    - Follow-up: "What would need to change?"

---

### Wrap-Up (2 minutes)

17. **"Is there anything about how you manage tasks that we didn't cover?"**

18. **"Any questions for me?"**

> "Thank you so much for your time. Your insights are incredibly valuable."

## Script B: Focused Minimalist (David Archetype)

### Participant Profile
- Values simplicity intentionally
- Has tried and rejected complex tools
- Likely has routines/rituals
- Prefers craft and quality

### Duration: 35 minutes

---

### Section 1: Warm-Up (5 minutes)

*Goal: Understand their minimalist philosophy*

> "Thanks for joining me today. I'm researching how people who value simplicity approach task management. I'd love to learn about your perspective."

**Questions:**

1. **"Tell me about yourself and what you do."**
   - Listen for: Intentionality, craft focus
   - Note: How they describe their work/life approach

2. **"How would you describe your approach to tools and technology?"**
   - Listen for: Minimalist language, curation
   - Follow-up: "Has that always been your approach?"

3. **"What does 'simple' mean to you when it comes to software?"**
   - Listen for: Their definition of simplicity
   - Note: This will be key to understanding their expectations

---

### Section 2: Tool Philosophy (10 minutes)

*Goal: Understand their rejection of complexity*

4. **"Tell me about a time you stopped using an app because it was too complex or bloated."**
   - Listen for: Specific frustrations, trigger moments
   - Follow-up: "What was the final straw?"

5. **"What's the difference between an app that's 'simple' and one that's 'lacking features'?"**
   - Listen for: Nuanced thinking about simplicity
   - Note: This reveals their sophistication

6. **"Describe a tool you love using. What makes it great?"**
   - Listen for: Quality indicators, emotional connection
   - Follow-up: "How did you find it?"

7. **"How do you feel about apps that try to 'help' you with suggestions, reminders, or notifications?"**
   - Listen for: Autonomy preference, notification fatigue
   - Follow-up: "When are notifications helpful vs. intrusive?"

**Hypothesis Validation:**

| Hypothesis | Question to Ask | Signal to Watch |
|------------|-----------------|-----------------|
| Actively rejects feature bloat | Q4 | Has specific examples of quitting apps |
| Values aesthetics | Q6 | Mentions design, typography, whitespace |
| Wants calm, not urgency | Q7 | Dislikes notifications, badges |
| Low task volume (3-5/day) | Q9 | Manages few, focused tasks |

---

### Section 3: Task Management Approach (10 minutes)

*Goal: Understand their rituals and patterns*

8. **"Walk me through how you plan your day."**
   - Listen for: Rituals, intentionality, time of day
   - Follow-up: "How long have you done it this way?"

9. **"How many tasks do you typically work on in a day?"**
   - Listen for: Low numbers, focus on quality
   - Follow-up: "How do you decide what makes the list?"

10. **"What happens to tasks you don't complete? How do you handle that?"**
    - Listen for: Review habits, letting go of tasks
    - Follow-up: "How do you feel when a task lingers?"

11. **"Do you have a 'cleanup' ritual for your task system?"**
    - Listen for: Weekly review, fresh starts
    - Follow-up: "What does that process look like?"

12. **"What role does paper play in your task management, if any?"**
    - Listen for: Analog appreciation, digital skepticism
    - Follow-up: "What do you like about paper?"

---

### Section 4: Aesthetic & Experience (5 minutes)

*Goal: Understand their quality bar*

13. **"When you open an app for the first time, what do you notice?"**
    - Listen for: Visual priorities, judgment process
    - Follow-up: "How quickly do you form an opinion?"

14. **"What makes software feel 'well-crafted' to you?"**
    - Listen for: Attention to detail, polish indicators
    - Note: Emotional language

15. **"How important is it that an app 'gets out of your way'?"**
    - Listen for: Friction sensitivity
    - Follow-up: "What does that look like in practice?"

---

### Section 5: Concept Reaction (Optional - 5 minutes)

*Show BMad Todo or mockup*

16. **"Take a look at this. What's your gut reaction?"**
    - Note: Immediate response, facial expression
    - Listen for: Simplicity recognition or concern

17. **"What would you expect to be able to do here?"**
    - Listen for: Mental model accuracy
    - Follow-up: "Is there anything missing that you'd need?"

18. **"Does this feel like a tool you would consider using?"**
    - Listen for: Honest evaluation
    - Follow-up: "What would make you choose this over your current approach?"

---

### Wrap-Up (2 minutes)

19. **"Is there anything about simplicity in tools that we didn't discuss?"**

20. **"Any questions for me?"**

> "Thank you so much. Your thoughtful perspective is exactly what I needed."

## Script C: Student Juggler (Jordan Archetype)

### Participant Profile
- Currently student or recent grad
- Multiple responsibilities
- High task volume
- Mobile-heavy
- Tech-savvy

### Duration: 35 minutes

---

### Section 1: Warm-Up (5 minutes)

*Goal: Understand their juggling context*

> "Thanks for chatting with me! I'm researching how students manage everything they have going on. I'd love to hear about your experience."

**Questions:**

1. **"Tell me about what you're juggling right now — school, work, activities, whatever."**
   - Listen for: Volume, variety, stress level
   - Note: Energy and enthusiasm

2. **"How do you feel about the amount you have on your plate?"**
   - Listen for: Overwhelm, coping strategies
   - Follow-up: "Has it always been like this?"

3. **"How would you describe yourself when it comes to organization and planning?"**
   - Listen for: Self-perception, aspirations
   - Follow-up: "What does your ideal organized self look like?"

---

### Section 2: Current Behavior (10 minutes)

*Goal: Understand high-volume task management*

4. **"Walk me through how you currently keep track of everything you need to do."**
   - Listen for: Tools, chaos, coping mechanisms
   - Follow-up: "Show me your system if you can."

5. **"Tell me about the last time you felt overwhelmed with tasks. What happened?"**
   - Listen for: Anxiety triggers, coping responses
   - Follow-up: "How did you get through it?"

6. **"How do you capture tasks when you're on the go — like walking between classes?"**
   - Listen for: Mobile behavior, speed
   - Follow-up: "What device? How long does it take?"

7. **"What happens when you think of something you need to do but can't capture it easily?"**
   - Listen for: Lost tasks, frustration
   - Follow-up: "How often does that happen?"

**Hypothesis Validation:**

| Hypothesis | Question to Ask | Signal to Watch |
|------------|-----------------|-----------------|
| High task volume (15-25/day) | Q4, Q5 | Mentions many tasks, multiple domains |
| Brain dump behavior | Q5 | Describes anxiety → capture → relief |
| Mobile capture critical | Q6 | Adds tasks on phone frequently |
| Speed is paramount | Q7 | Gets frustrated with slow capture |

---

### Section 3: Mobile Experience (8 minutes)

*Goal: Deep dive on mobile task capture*

8. **"What percentage of your tasks do you add from your phone vs. computer?"**
   - Listen for: Mobile dominance
   - Follow-up: "Why do you think that is?"

9. **"Describe the ideal mobile task capture. What would it feel like?"**
   - Listen for: Speed, simplicity, one-handed use
   - Note: Emotional language

10. **"Have you ever been frustrated trying to add a task on your phone?"**
    - Listen for: Specific UX friction points
    - Follow-up: "What was the problem?"

11. **"How important is it for your app to work when WiFi is bad or you're offline?"**
    - Listen for: Reliability needs
    - Follow-up: "Tell me about a time connectivity was a problem."

---

### Section 4: Completion & Progress (5 minutes)

*Goal: Understand batch completion and motivation*

12. **"How do you feel when you check off tasks on your list?"**
    - Listen for: Emotional reward, motivation
    - Follow-up: "Is that feeling important to you?"

13. **"Do you ever have sessions where you check off a bunch of tasks at once?"**
    - Listen for: Batch completion behavior
    - Follow-up: "What triggers that?"

14. **"How do you know if you're making progress on your tasks?"**
    - Listen for: Progress indicators, counting
    - Follow-up: "Would a 'completed count' be useful?"

---

### Section 5: Tool Preferences (5 minutes)

*Goal: Understand tech-savvy preferences*

15. **"What apps do you use most frequently? What do you like about them?"**
    - Listen for: Modern app preferences, speed
    - Note: Brands and patterns they mention

16. **"Have you ever looked at the source code of an app you use?"**
    - Listen for: Developer curiosity
    - Follow-up: "What would make you do that?"

17. **"What would make you recommend a task app to your friends?"**
    - Listen for: Viral triggers, social proof
    - Follow-up: "What would make you NOT recommend it?"

---

### Section 6: Concept Reaction (Optional - 5 minutes)

*Show BMad Todo or mockup*

18. **"Here's a simple task app — take a look and tell me what you think."**
    - Note: Immediate reaction, engagement
    - Follow-up: "What do you notice first?"

19. **"Try adding 5 tasks as fast as you can."**
    - Note: Time to complete, flow
    - Follow-up: "How did that feel? Fast enough?"

20. **"Would you use this? Would you tell friends about it?"**
    - Listen for: Honest assessment, sharing likelihood

---

### Wrap-Up (2 minutes)

21. **"Anything about being a busy student that we didn't cover?"**

22. **"Any questions for me?"**

> "Thanks so much! Good luck with everything you have going on."
